Karaikudi is the largest city of Sivaganga district in the Indian state of Tamil Nadu. It is the centre of the Karaikudi Metropolitan Area, the 21st largest urban Agglomeration of Tamil Nadu based on 2011 census data. It is part of the area commonly referred to as Chettinad and has been declared a City Municipal Corporation by the Government of Tamil Nadu,on account of the palatial houses built with limestone called Kaarai veedu. which undergoes major developments as part of Government Schemes

HISTORICAL MILESTONES:

The city derives its name from the thorny plant Karai, referred to in ancient literature as Karaikudi. The town was established in the 19th century, and the oldest known structure is the Koppudaiya Nayagi Amman Temple. Mahatma Gandhi delivered two speeches in Karaikudi in 1927 and Bharathiyar visited Karaikudi in 1919 to participate in an event. After independence, the city saw significant growth in the industrial sector. Karaikudi and surrounding areas are generally referred as "Chettinad". Chettinad comprises a network of 73 villages and 2 towns forming clusters spread over a territory of 1,550 km2 in the districts of Sivagangai and Pudukottai in the state of Tamil Nadu. Karaikudi is the largest town in Sivaganga district. Karaikudi and surrounding areas are very popular for their unique palaces, Chettinad Architecture based buildings and palatial mansions. Kannadasan Manimandapam, Kamban Manimandapam and Thousand windows house are other visitor attractions in Karaikudi.

Architecture

Spaciousness and Courtyards:

Chettinad houses are known for their expansive layouts, often with multiple courtyards, verandas, and spacious rooms designed to accommodate large families and social gatherings.

Intricate Woodwork:

Elaborate carvings on doors, pillars, and furniture are a hallmark of this style, often using Burma teak.

Imported Materials:

They incorporated materials from their international trade, including Italian marble, Belgian glass, and Sri Lankan tiles, alongside local materials like granite and limestone.

Vividly Painted Athangudi Tiles:

These handcrafted tiles, made in the village of Athangudi, are a distinctive feature, adding vibrant colors and patterns to floors and walls.

Cooling Techniques:

The thick walls, courtyards, and verandas were designed to provide natural ventilation and maintain comfortable interior temperatures in the hot, dry climate.

Layout and Design:

The houses typically follow a grid-like layout centered around a courtyard, with rooms arranged around it and often featuring a raised platform (thinnai) for entertaining guests.

Cultural Significance:

The architecture reflects their social organization, with separate spaces for men and women, and their strong emphasis on community and hospitality.
